Job Coach — Product Framework

Phase 1: Product Framework

A structured foundation for an AI career coach serving early- and mid-career professionals across the UAE and GCC — bilingual, privacy-first, and built to keep a hard line between a confirmed fact and a guess.

Status Approved (amended 13 Jul 2026) Date 13 July 2026 Phase 0 Approved Next Phase 2 — Technical & AI Architecture
Part A — Foundation

Vision, problem, and who this is for

01 Product vision

A trusted AI career companion for the UAE and GCC that helps early- and mid-career professionals understand their own value, close the gap to the roles they actually want, and apply with confidence — through a profile that keeps learning and a coach that explains itself.

02 Product mission

Replace generic resume templates and stateless chatbot advice with career guidance grounded in each user's real, confirmed history — transparent about what it knows, what it's inferring, and what it still needs to ask.

03 Core problem statement

Job seekers hunt across disconnected surfaces — CV, LinkedIn, job boards, generic AI tools, memory — none of which talk to each other. Generic AI writes generic resumes because it doesn't know the person. Job boards match on keywords, not fit. Rejection arrives with no explanation. Career advice, when it exists, isn't personalized and forgets last week's conversation.

04 Target audience

Early-career (0–3 yrs) and mid-career (4–15 yrs) professionals, actively searching or weighing a transition, based in or targeting roles across the UAE and wider GCC, working in English and/or Arabic, split across mobile and desktop.

05 Primary personas

Ahmed, 24 — early-career engineer, Dubai. One internship, a degree that doesn't map cleanly to the job titles he sees posted, applying broadly with a low response rate. Doesn't know which resume gaps are actually disqualifying versus imagined.
Fatima, 32 — mid-career marketing lead, Abu Dhabi, weighing a pivot toward product/digital marketing. Eight years of experience she struggles to reframe in a new field's vocabulary; family and location constraints narrow her options; wants a structured skills-gap view, not another generic "upskill" list.

06 Secondary personas

  • Career switcher leaving a contracting industry, retraining mid-search.
  • Professional returning after a career break, rebuilding confidence and a narrative for the gap.
  • Expat professional navigating visa sponsorship and work-authorization constraints as a hard filter on every search.

07 Jobs to be done

  • "When I don't know what roles I'm actually qualified for, tell me honestly so I stop wasting applications."
  • "When I've been rejected repeatedly, help me understand why without guessing."
  • "When I update one part of my story, don't make me repeat the rest of it."
  • "When a job looks good on paper, tell me what I'm missing before I apply."
  • "When I don't know what to learn next, connect it to a role I actually want."

08 User pain points

Fragmented profile across platforms · generic AI-written resumes that overstate or invent · no visibility into why a match was suggested · repetitive, ungrounded interview questions from other tools · unclear which preference is actually blocking matches · course recommendations disconnected from a real target role · rejection without feedback · advice that ignores regional hiring norms.

09 Value proposition

One profile, built once and corrected as it grows, that a coach actually uses — not a chatbot that forgets every session, not a job board that only reads keywords. Every recommendation says which fact it's based on and what's still a guess.

10 Key differentiators

  • Persistent structured profile with fact/inference provenance, not a stateless chat window.
  • Explainable matching — every score comes with a reason and a gap list, not a black-box percentage.
  • UAE/GCC-first, bilingual EN/AR from day one, not a global product with translation bolted on.
  • Coach, profile, and job discovery share one data model, not three tools wearing one skin.
  • Hard rule against fabrication — nothing is invented that isn't in the profile.

11 Core user journeys

Onboarding & profile creation → guided career interview → first coach conversation and recommendation → browsing and saving job matches → (post-MVP) tailoring an application → ongoing check-ins as preferences and the market shift.

Part B — Scope

What's in the MVP, what waits

12 Feature inventory

Full inventory with phase tagging is in the feature priority matrix and MoSCoW table below.

13 MVP features confirmed pillars

Account creation & auth · CV/document upload with structured extraction · manual profile entry & correction · fact/inference/missing labeling · guided career interview (personalized, skippable, resumable) · AI career coach (conversational, explainable, cites profile data) · job discovery & matching from at least one feasible source · save/dismiss jobs · basic dashboard · consent controls, data export & delete · English + Arabic UI.

Everything in the MVP ships free. No billing, payment processing, or tiering/usage-gating infrastructure is built for this release — that's a deliberate scope cut, not an oversight.

14 Post-MVP features

Resume tailoring per job · cover letter drafting · application tracking & reminders · mock interviews · salary negotiation guidance · course/learning recommendations · networking & personal branding guidance · notifications · calendar integration · admin tooling · expanded job-source coverage · native mobile app · paid subscription tier & billing infrastructure.

15 Explicitly excluded from MVP

Automatic application submission · autonomous outreach to recruiters or employers · any LinkedIn/Indeed integration beyond user-provided exports · paid course-marketplace integrations · native mobile apps · multi-role admin system.

Part C — Requirements

What the system must do, and where AI stops

16 Functional requirements

  • Auth & account management
  • Document upload & parsing into the profile schema
  • Profile CRUD with a correction interface
  • Consent toggles per data category
  • Conversational AI interface
  • Job listing display, save/dismiss, status tracking
  • Machine-readable data export and full account/data deletion

17 Non-functional requirements

Conversational, streaming-where-possible AI responses · stable at pilot scale (10–50 concurrent users) without a redesign · encryption in transit and at rest · UAE PDPL data-residency considered · WCAG 2.1 AA target · cost-aware AI usage with token budgets per tier.

18 AI capabilities

Document extraction & structuring · personalized interview question generation · coaching conversation grounded in stored profile data · job match scoring with plain-language explanation · skill-gap identification against a target role.

19 AI limitations

Never invents qualifications, titles, employers, achievements, or numbers. Cannot guarantee an outcome. Cannot submit applications, message recruiters, or take any external action without explicit per-action confirmation. Cannot access a platform without an authorized integration or user-provided export. Not a licensed source of legal, immigration, or financial advice. Match quality is bounded by whatever job-source data actually exists — gaps are shown, never silently filled.

20 Data requirements

Uploaded CV/resume files, certificates, portfolio/work samples · manually entered profile fields · interview answers · job interaction history · consent records with timestamps and versions.

Part D — Strategy

Where the data comes from, and how it's held

21 Data-source strategy

The user is the primary source of truth: uploads and manual entry first, a confirmation loop second. The system never scrapes. Licensed or official feeds are a secondary source, evaluated for cost and terms before use — never assumed available.

22 Job-discovery strategy

Sequencing for MVP: (1) user-provided saved links for personal tracking — zero integration risk, works day one; (2) evaluate free-tier or low-cost licensed aggregator APIs feasible for the GCC region — a concrete build/buy decision for Phase 2; (3) official company career-page feeds where structured data exists. No unauthorized scraping of LinkedIn, Indeed, Bayt, GulfTalent, NaukriGulf, or Glassdoor, at any point.

23 Course-recommendation strategy post-MVP

Most learning platforms expose no public matching API. V1: a curated skill-gap → provider search-link mapping (linking out, not embedding), evolving to direct integration only where a provider offers acceptable terms.

24 User-profile structure

Identity & Contact · Employment History · Education · Certifications · Skills (technical/soft) · Achievements & Responsibilities · Career Preferences (title, industry, location, work model, hours, salary, visa/authorization, relocation, contract type) · Career Goals · Skill Gaps · AI-Inferred Notes (structurally separate from confirmed fields) · Interaction History.

25 AI memory model

Four tiers: Permanent Profile (confirmed, user-approved facts) · Preferences (re-confirmed periodically — salary, location, visa status, availability, target roles, seniority, relocation) · Session Context (conversation-only, discarded unless promoted) · Sensitive Data (visa status, salary, personal constraints — flagged, minimal retention, explicit opt-in). Every field carries a provenance tag: Confirmed User-Stated AI-Inferred Needs Verification

Part E — Trust & Governance

Consent, safety, privacy, and access

26 Permissions & consent model

Granular per-category opt-in · full visibility into what's stored · edit or delete any field · export the whole profile · consent is versioned and logged, never a one-time checkbox.

27 Trust & safety model

Every recommendation cites the profile fact(s) behind it. No autonomous external action, ever, without explicit per-action confirmation — no auto-apply, no auto-send. Uploaded documents pass through a sandboxed pipeline with prompt-injection defenses: text extracted from a résumé is data, never an instruction to the assistant.

28 Privacy & security requirements

Encryption in transit and at rest · least-privilege access to documents and profile data · defined retention and deletion windows · access and audit logging · data residency evaluated against UAE PDPL. This is a framework position, not legal advice — a compliance review is recommended before handling live user data, especially visa and salary fields.

29 Accessibility requirements

WCAG 2.1 AA as the MVP target · full keyboard navigation · screen-reader-compatible forms, critical for the upload and correction flows · sufficient contrast in both themes · scalable text.

30 Localization requirements

English and Arabic UI at launch · true RTL layout support, not mirrored icons · AI coaching content generated natively per language rather than machine-translated after the fact · AED and regional date formatting.

Part F — Business

Model, pricing, and how success is measured

31 Business model options MVP: free, no monetization

The first release ships entirely free — no billing surface, no gating. Monetization is deliberately deferred to the next release, once the pilot shows what people actually use. When it's introduced: Freemium subscription remains the recommended direction. B2B2C licensing to universities, career centers, or government employment programs is a strong long-term fit for the GCC market, worth exploring post-pilot. Pure pay-per-feature is not recommended — mismatched to an ongoing coaching relationship.

32 Pricing considerations deferred to next release

Not part of this release. Kept here only as a forward-looking reference so it doesn't need re-deriving later: comparable AI career tools price roughly AED 35–75/month (~US$10–20) for a paid tier. Actual pricing should be set from pilot usage and cost data, not from this estimate alone — revisit after the free pilot has run.

33 Success metrics

Profile completion rate · career-interview completion rate · coach engagement (sessions/week, messages/session) · job-match save rate · self-reported "applied" and, longer-term, "landed a role" via periodic check-ins · free-to-paid conversion · retention/churn.

34 Product analytics

Funnel drop-off through onboarding · feature usage by tier · thumbs-up/down on AI recommendations · AI cost per active user · job-source click-through — all behind the same consent model as everything else, nothing tracked silently.

Part G — Risk & Planning

What could go wrong, and the path from here

35–36 Key risks & mitigations

Full register with likelihood and impact is in the risk register table below. Headline risk: the 3-month solo timeline is achievable only if scope holds to the three confirmed MVP pillars.

37 Assumptions consolidated

  • Pilot-stage Claude API cost of roughly $50–300/month for 10–50 active users, pending real usage telemetry — matters more now that there's no revenue offsetting it during the free pilot.
  • At least one GCC-relevant job source will be feasible at free or low cost — pending Phase 2 research, not guaranteed.
  • Even with no paid tier, usage limits (e.g. coach messages/month, CV parses) are still likely needed to keep AI cost bounded during the free pilot — sized once real usage data exists, not guessed now.

38 Open decisions not blocking

Product name, domain, and brand identity · exact usage limits for the free pilot · final job-source integration(s) · PDPL compliance review outcome · native app timing · when and how to introduce the paid tier for the next release.

39 MVP acceptance criteria

A user can: register and log in · upload a CV and see it converted into a structured profile · correct any extracted field · complete a guided interview that skips what's already known · have a coach conversation and receive a recommendation naming the profile fact behind it · see job matches from at least one real source with a plain-language reason and save/dismiss action · export or delete their entire profile · use the product fully in English or Arabic.

40 Recommended roadmap

Weeks 1–4
Foundation — auth, profile schema, CV upload & extraction, correction UI.
Weeks 5–8
Intelligence — guided interview engine, AI coach conversation loop, fact/inference tagging.
Weeks 9–12
Discovery & pilot — one job-source integration, save/dismiss + basic dashboard, ship to a small pilot group, start collecting real usage and cost data.
Month 4+
Iterate — expand job sources, add application support and course recommendations, revisit native mobile.

This is aggressive but achievable for a solo, AI-assisted, non-technical founder only if scope holds to the three MVP pillars — profile creation, AI coach, job discovery/matching. Anything extra comes out of Month 4+, not out of the pillars.
